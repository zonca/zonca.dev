CloudBank provides a simplified way for researchers and educators to access commercial cloud resources, including Amazon Web Services (AWS) and Google Cloud.

## Who is Eligible?

Access to AWS and Google Cloud resources via CloudBank is available to:

- Researchers and educators affiliated with U.S. academic institutions (including faculty, staff, postdocs, and students).
- Principal Investigators (PIs) with active research projects, especially those funded by the National Science Foundation (NSF) or other federal agencies.
- Collaborators on eligible research projects.

If you are unsure about your eligibility, contact your institution’s research computing office or CloudBank support.

## How to Request Access

1. **Prepare your project information:** Gather details about your research project, including objectives, collaborators, and anticipated cloud resource needs.
2. **Visit the CloudBank Request Portal:** Go to [CloudBank Request Access](https://www.cloudbank.org/request-access-commercial-cloud-resources).
3. **Complete the online request form:** Provide your institutional affiliation, project description, and any required documentation.
4. **Approval process:** Your request may require approval from your institution or project PI. CloudBank staff will review your submission and may contact you for additional information.
5. **Account setup:** Once approved, you will receive instructions for accessing AWS or Google Cloud resources through CloudBank.

## Limitations and Requirements

- Access is typically limited to research and educational projects; commercial or personal use is not permitted.
- Some projects may require evidence of funding or institutional approval.
- Usage may be subject to quotas, budget limits, or compliance requirements set by your institution or funding agency.
- Users must comply with the acceptable use policies of both CloudBank and the respective cloud provider.

## Use Cases and Benefits

CloudBank is particularly useful for:

- Small- to medium-scale research production infrastructure.
- AI and machine learning workloads requiring scalable compute or GPU resources.
- Data analysis, storage, and sharing for collaborative research.
- Teaching courses that require access to cloud computing environments.

Benefits include:

- Simplified account management and billing.
- Access to technical support and training resources.
- Centralized tracking of cloud usage for research reporting.

For questions or assistance, contact CloudBank support at [support@cloudbank.org](mailto:support@cloudbank.org) or consult your institution’s research computing office.
